Tips for using Wood Fired Ovens

  • Firing your oven: start the fire in the oven approximately hour and a half before cooking. Use about 8-9 logs of wood in the center of the oven to start the fire, after the oven has reached the desired temperature, push the logs to the back and start cooking!
  • It is important to remember that any kind of fire starter that has an unpleasant odor or toxic chemicals should not be used, as it will leave a bad smell and taste in your oven and food.
  • Chemically treated wood should not be used.
  • Avoid feeding too much wood into the oven as this will burn the food.
  • Every time you use your oven burn it with ample air flow, by leaving the chimney and door open, to heat up the chimney and crystallize the tars.
  • Wood fired ovens, are pretty much self-cleaning. The fire and the heat burn off pretty much everything, from fallen food particles to oil and grease stains. Once the oven is cold, all you have to do is gently brush out the ashes and the pizza oven is ready to be fired up again!
  • You can use the same variety of metal, terracotta, ceramic and other oven proof pans that you use in your regular oven, provided you don’t place them directly by the flame.
  • Always burn dry seasoned wood, never fresh or wet wood.
  • If the wood produces a lot of black smoke, it is generally an indication that you are using the wrong wood.
  • Use a damp cloth to clean the oven walls from the outside. This should be done at least twice a week to avoid accumulation of dust on the oven.
  • Do not use cleaning agents or fluids (other than water) to clean the cooking surface.
  • When the oven is not in use, keep the door and chimney closed.
